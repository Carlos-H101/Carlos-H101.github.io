1st Place — $3,000 Award

Miners Pitch Competition

Mike Loya Center · UTEP · November 2025

Selected from over 60 applications to pitch The Sense Cube — a wireless handheld device that translates real-time smartphone messages into tactile Braille via six high-precision solenoid pins.

The pitch focused on a clear story: accessibility-first hardware built end-to-end as a student. We walked judges through the working prototype, the embedded firmware, and the path from breadboard to enclosure. After advancing through every elimination round, the project took 1st place against a strong UTEP cohort.

The award covered $3,000 in prize funding directed toward continuing development — sourcing higher-precision solenoids, refining the Bluetooth stack, and investigating a smaller PCB respin.
